[Theory] Spherical Waves under Paraxial and Far-Field Conditions

Mathematical derivation concerning spherical waves under paraxial and far-field conditions

287 words & characters
1 minute read

Figure 1 sketches the basic hypothesis of the author’s theory. In the hypothesis, Q is the source point that emits spherical waves2 constantly in the universe while P is the field point in considerable distance that receive electromagnetic waves emitted by Q.
Suppose both Q and P can be described in space rectangular coordinate system as follows.$$Q(x_0,y_0,0)\tag{1}$$$$P(x,y,z)\tag{2}$$$$\left|PQ\right|=r\tag{3}$$

Q is the source point that emits spherical waves, which can be described as $$\vec{E}(P)=\frac{A}{\vec{r}}\exp{(\mathrm{i}k\vec{r})}\tag{4}$$ Suppose A=1 in (4) and E, r are scalar, we have$$E(P)=\frac{1}{r}\exp{(\mathrm{i}kr)}\tag{5}$$ According to the Pythagorean theorem, r can be described as $$r=\sqrt{(x-x_0)^2+(y-y_0)^2+z^2}$$$$r=z\sqrt{\frac{(x-x_0)^2+(y-y_0)^2}{z^2}+1}$$ With the help of binomial expansion, we have$$r=z (1+\frac{(x-x_0)^2+(y-y_0)^2}{2z^2}-\frac{[(x-x_0)^2+(y-y_0)^2]^2}{8z^4}+\cdots ) \tag{6}$$ Given the paraxial condition, we have$$z^2\gg [(x-x_0)^2+(y-y_0)^2]_{\max}\tag{7}$$ Given the far-field condition, we have$$k \frac{x^2+y^2}{2z} \ll \pi $$$$k \frac{x_0^2+y_0^2}{2z} \ll \pi $$i.e.$$\frac{x^2+y^2}{2z}\ll \frac{\lambda}{2}\tag{8}$$$$\frac{x_0^2+y_0^2}{2z}\ll \frac{\lambda}{2}\tag{9}$$ 2 parts in (5) with different colours are displayed below.$$E(P)=\frac{1}{\color{pink}r}\exp(\mathrm{i}k{\color{lightblue}r})$$ In that the wave number k of red light is large, we take zero order approximation to the pink part and first order approximation to the lightblue part. And (9) is also the result of paraxial condition. $$E(P)=\frac{1}{\color{pink}z}\exp \mathrm{i}k\left[ {\color{lightblue}z+\frac{(x-x_0)^2+(y-y_0)^2}{2z}} \right] \tag{10}$$ In view of$$xx_0\ll z\tag{11}$$$$yy_0\ll z\tag{12}$$ we can derive (13) from (8), (9), (11) and (12).$$E(P)=\frac{1}{z}\exp (\mathrm{i}kz)\tag{13}$$ Above expression (13) of spherical waves in Q is the form of plane wave. We come to the conclusion that spherical waves under paraxial and far-field conditions can be approximately regarded as plane wave.
